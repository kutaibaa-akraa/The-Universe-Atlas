An Atlas of the Universe - Links

This is a list of web sites which contain maps and data about the universe on various scales. If you were brought to this links page by a search engine and you are looking for maps try my home page first. :)

  • General Sites
    • Powers of Ten by Bruce Bryson is a website which tries to zoom in from the universe to sub-atomic scales.
    • Celestia is a computer program that allows you to fly through the universe from the solar system to beyond the Galaxy.
    • The Universe in 3D is a Java applet written by Are Uppman which allows you to zoom around the universe.
    • Knowing the Universe and its Secrets by Norbert Rumiano is an introduction to astronomy by the person who translated my website into French.
  • Nearest Stars
    • 3-D Starmaps by Winchell Chung contains information and advice about constructing 3D starmaps.
    • SolStation.com contains lots of detailed information about many of the nearest stars.
    • The Astronomy Nexus by David Nash has an Encyclopedia of Suns which describes many of the Sun-like stars within 50 light years.
    • The Internet Stellar Database by Roger Wilcox provides information about many of the stars within 75 light years.
    • NASA's NStars Database is a database of over 2600 stars within 25 parsecs (82 light years).
  • The Milky Way Galaxy
    • Star Trek Kartographie is a Star Trek site which contains some maps scanned from 'The Guide to the Galaxy' by N Henbest and H Couper.
    • The Spitzer Space Telescope team have produce their own artist's impression of the Milky Way viewed from above.
    • Galaxy Map by Kevin Jardine presents a map of many of the thousands of stars, clusters, and nebulae in the Milky Way.
  • The Local Group
    • The Local Group of Galaxies by H Frommert and C Kronberg contains information and pictures about the galaxies in the Local Group.
    • The Extended Local Group of Galaxies is an article about the Local Group (and the Sculptor and Maffei groups) by Rami Rekola in his Encyclopædia Galactica.
  • Virgo Supercluster
    • Nearby Groups of Galaxies is an article about the Virgo Supercluster written by Gerard de Vaucouleurs in 1975.
    • Galaxies in VRML by Honeylocust Media Systems allows you to fly around the Virgo Supercluster. (A VRML plug-in is needed).
    • Large Scale Structure of the Universe on Brent Tully's website contains various images of the Virgo supercluster and some nearby superclusters.
    • This article about the Virgo supercluster was written by Brent Tully in the Astrophysical Journal in 1982. It contains some maps of the supercluster.
  • Superclusters
    • Superclusters, Observed Properties by Anthony Fairall describes some of the closest superclusters.
    • The Large Scale Structure of the Universe by Andrey Kravtsov contains plots of galaxies out to about 300 million light years.
    • The Local Universe in 3D by Mike Hudson contains some 3D plots of the nearest superclusters and an MPEG movie called Powers of Two.
    • Observing Galaxy Clusters by Jim Shields (on his Adventures in Deep Space page) explains how you can observe nearby superclusters with a small telescope.
    • Allsky Images of the XSC contains some all-sky plots of the 1.6 million galaxies from the 2MASS Extended Source Catalog (XSC).
  • The Universe
    • The Sloan Digital Sky Survey aims to plot the positions of a million galaxies out to several billion light years.
    • The 2dF Galaxy Redshift Survey has plotted the positions of 250 000 galaxies in two directions to about 3.5 billion light years.
Link Directories
AstronomyLinks.Com is a large directory of astronomy and space links.
AstroWeb is a large collection of astronomy links used by professional astronomers.
Astronomy Homework Help by Gary Agranat contains links for lots of astronomy subjects at all levels of expertise.
Online Catalogues
The CDS Service has huge amounts of astronomy catalogues provided by professional astronomers.
The NASA Astrophysics Data System is a huge archive of astronomy papers.
Messier45.com lets you generate lists of galaxies, nebulae or clusters from lots of catalogues.
